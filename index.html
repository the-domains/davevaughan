LIGHT

Of all the senses available to us limited, yet blessed beings on this planet, sight is my must have. I’d hate to lose any sensation this flesh affords, but the daily dose of light I chase as a photographer, which lights my way, warms my back, falls just so on the landscape, the structure, the figure, is what I crave. It’s all about the light. The sun makes it happen, literally all day. I just chase it.

BALANCE

When you only have four walls to your photo, you must make some decisions. What shall fill each corner? More importantly at times, is what is best left out of focus, out of the frame, so that what matters comes to the surface of the image. The first impression. The first two seconds a pair of eyes scan an image, is when they will decide if there is a sense of calm, a hint of safety with which to rest a lingering gaze.

PEOPLE

More than a setting sun; more than a rising tide against a dawn-lit island beach; people do it for me as a photographer. Sure, I want to revisit and escape back into those landscape photos I took back when, but I do prefer the human connection more when I’m making images. It’s a collaboration. It’s magic when it works and we both flip the switch, pull the trigger, at just the right time.

Mask Me Nicely

This series explores the power of heightened personal expression–behind the comfort of anonymity. It’s about art and its relationship to our everyday reality. If art is an escape, a transcendence from the mundane, a departure from the mere drudgery of daily existence, then adding a bit of mystery can only help the process.
